# Supply Chain, Capacity, and Order Analysis

Use this reference for manufacturing, cyclical, hardware, robotics, semiconductor, EV, energy, commodity, and thematic ETF analysis.

## Supply-Chain Map

Build a concise map before making a recommendation:

| Layer | What to check | Why it matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Upstream inputs | Raw materials, key components, equipment, energy, labor | Cost pressure, bottlenecks, margin sensitivity |
| Core company / ETF holdings | Product mix, technology moat, unit economics, capacity, utilization | Determines earnings leverage and competitive edge |
| Downstream demand | End customers, industry capex, replacement cycle, policy subsidies, export demand | Determines order visibility and cycle durability |
| Channels and integrators | Distributors, system integrators, OEM/ODM relationships | Affects pricing power and working capital |
| Substitutes and competitors | Domestic/import substitution, alternative technologies | Affects long-term valuation and margins |

## Capacity and Utilization Checklist

For each important company or sector bucket, collect as much as practical:

- Designed capacity vs effective capacity.
- Utilization rate and trend.
- New capacity projects, capex, ramp dates, and delay risk.
- Order backlog, book-to-bill, delivery cycle, and customer concentration.
- Inventory days, receivable days, prepayments, and contract liabilities.
- Gross margin trend and whether margin changes come from price, cost, mix, or scale.
- Unit economics: ASP, unit cost, yield, labor efficiency, energy cost, and depreciation.
- Supply-demand balance: whether new industry capacity is likely to create oversupply.

## Robotics-Specific Buckets

When analyzing robotics stocks or ETFs, separate the value chain:

1. **AI / software / machine vision**: models, perception, planning, control algorithms, industrial software.
2. **Sensors and vision hardware**: cameras, LiDAR, force/torque sensors, encoders.
3. **Controllers and servo systems**: motion control, servo motors, drives, PLCs.
4. **Reducers and transmission**: harmonic reducers, RV reducers, precision transmission.
5. **Actuators and joints**: integrated joints, linear actuators, dexterous hands.
6. **Robot OEMs**: industrial robots, collaborative robots, service robots, humanoid robots.
7. **Automation integrators**: factory automation, system integration, downstream production lines.
8. **Downstream demand**: autos, 3C electronics, lithium battery, photovoltaics, logistics, healthcare, domestic substitution, humanoid pilot projects.

## ETF / Theme Analysis

For thematic ETFs:

- Break top holdings into the buckets above and estimate concentration by bucket.
- Identify whether returns are driven by actual orders/profits or by theme valuation expansion.
- Compare fund valuation with expected earnings growth of major holdings.
- Check scale, liquidity, tracking error, premium/discount, and whether high turnover could hurt execution.
- For buy/sell plans, use ETF technical levels, not single-stock prices; for thesis, use constituent supply-chain and order evidence.

## Red Flags

- Capacity expansion faster than demand, causing price wars.
- High revenue growth but rising receivables/inventory and weak operating cash flow.
- Orders concentrated in one customer or policy subsidy.
- Product demos or pilot orders without mass-production evidence.
- Theme valuation far ahead of earnings visibility.
- Gross margin falling while management still announces aggressive capacity expansion.
